About

Planner. Researcher.
Spatial analyst.

I'm Umme Laiba Raha, a Master of Urban and Environmental Planning (MUEP) candidate at Arizona State University (expected May 2026, GPA 3.95). My work sits at the intersection of housing equity, urban heat resilience, GIS, and community development.

At ASU I serve as both a Graduate Teaching Assistant — supporting 300+ students across GIS 211/311, Planning Methods, Sustainable Cities, and Global Change — and a Graduate Service Assistant for CEE 281 Surveying, teaching field measurement techniques with total stations and auto levels.

Currently I work as a Planning, Community and Economic Development Associate at aLocal.ai, applying LLMs and spatial data analysis to equitable economic planning. My most formative applied experience was a GIS internship at the Central Arizona Project — Arizona's single largest renewable water supply system — maintaining the enterprise geodatabase and conducting field data collection along the canal.

I am originally from Bangladesh, where I earned my B.Sc. in Geography and Environmental Studies from the University of Chittagong (top 1%, GPA 3.83), and where my interest in e-waste, flood resilience, and environmental justice first took root.
